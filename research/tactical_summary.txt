================================================================================
  2025 MISS INVESTIGATION
================================================================================

Delta to baseline in 2025 (IS):  -19.80pp  (worst year in the sample)

ROOT CAUSE:
  Gold rallied strongly in 2025 while DFII10 was ABOVE its 90-day SMA for much
  of the year. The CvsTC signal was therefore OFF, parking the upper gold tranche
  in cash while gold gained ~25–30%.

  This was not a lag cost issue. It was a genuine regime mismatch: gold in 2025
  was driven by central-bank buying, geopolitical demand, and USD weakness —
  factors not reflected in the real-yield trend signal.

  60-day SMA did not help; the signal was also OFF during the same periods.

KEY INSIGHT: The signal was *technically correct* about real yields — DFII10 was
not below trend. Gold simply decoupled from real yields in 2025. This is a known
risk of yield-based gold signals and is hard to fully hedge within the same
framework. The floor and compress additions below partially address it.


================================================================================
  FLOOR OPTIMISATION
================================================================================

TEST: Sweep gold minimum floor from 0% to 25% in 2.5% steps (11 values).
  floor = 0.0%  →  original tactical (0% when OFF, 25% when ON)
  floor = 25.0% →  fully deployed baseline (always 25%)
  gold_w = floor + (25% - floor) × signal

FINDING: There is no interior optimum. IS and OOS Sharpe move in strict
opposite directions across all floor values:

  Floor     IS Sharpe   OOS Sharpe   Combined
  0.0%        1.923       1.058        2.981   ← best OOS
  2.5%        1.933       1.049        2.982   ← best combined
  12.5%       1.963       1.008        2.971
  25.0%       1.981       0.945        2.926   ← best IS

CONCLUSION: A floor alone is just a continuous dial between "pure tactical"
and "always deployed." It does not add a new degree of freedom. The optimal
combined IS+OOS score peaks near 0–5% floor, essentially the original signal.


================================================================================
  COMBINED SIGNAL OPTIMISATION
================================================================================

TEST: Add a "compressing real yields" trigger alongside CvsTC.
  Combined gold signal = CvsTC  OR  (DFII10 today < DFII10 N days ago)

Rationale: Even when yields are ABOVE their 90-day trend (CvsTC OFF), if they
are actively falling over N days, gold tends to benefit. This specifically
targets the 2025 failure mode — yields were above trend but falling.

SWEEP: 5 floors × 4 compress windows = 20 combinations
  Floors:   0%, 12.5%, 15%, 17.5%, 20%
  Windows:  0 (CvsTC only), 10d, 20d, 30d

KEY FINDING: The compress trigger improves BOTH IS and OOS simultaneously —
unlike the floor sweep which was a pure IS/OOS tradeoff.

FULL RESULTS — best configs by combined IS+OOS Sharpe:
  Config                    ISSh    OOSSh   Combined  ISCAGR  ISMaxDD
  CvsTC+10d / fl=0.0%      1.992   1.091    3.082    24.70%  -17.14%
  CvsTC+20d / fl=0.0%      1.968   1.098    3.066    24.36%  -16.99%
  CvsTC+10d / fl=12.5%     1.995   1.022    3.016    25.02%  -17.54%
  CvsTC+20d / fl=12.5%     1.982   1.026    3.008    24.85%  -17.54%
  CvsTC+10d / fl=15.0%     1.993   1.007    3.000    25.08%  -17.65%

FOCUS: 15–20% floor range (adds downside protection, reduces cash drag)
  Config                    ISSh    OOSSh   Combined
  fl=15% / CvsTC only      1.968   0.996    2.965
  fl=15% / CvsTC+10d       1.993   1.007    3.000  ← BEST in range
  fl=15% / CvsTC+20d       1.983   1.010    2.993
  fl=17.5% / CvsTC+10d     1.991   0.992    2.983
  fl=20% / CvsTC+10d       1.989   0.976    2.965

YEAR-BY-YEAR 2025 (IS) — how much the compress trigger helps:
  Baseline (fully deployed):     55.71%
  fl=15% / CvsTC only:           51.79%   (-3.92pp vs baseline)
  fl=15% / CvsTC+20d:            52.99%   (-2.72pp vs baseline)
  fl=20% / CvsTC+20d:            54.35%   (-1.36pp vs baseline)

  The compress trigger recovers ~1–2.5pp of the 2025 gap. It does not fully
  close it — the phase where yields were above trend AND not falling day-over-day
  vs N days ago still keeps the upper tranche in cash.

================================================================================
  ACTIONABLE CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

1. IMPLEMENT THE COMBINED SIGNAL
   Replace the binary CvsTC trigger with:
     fire = (DFII10 < 90d SMA)  OR  (DFII10 < DFII10[10 trading days ago])
   Set gold floor at 15% (not 0%). Upper 10% tranche remains signal-gated.
   This is a drop-in change to the existing tactical framework.

2. ACCEPT THE 2025 COST AS STRUCTURAL, NOT A BUG
   The 2025 miss (-3 to -4pp vs baseline) is not fully preventable within a
   yield-based framework. Gold decoupled from real yields. A 15% floor + compress
   window reduces this cost to ~-2.7pp; further reduction requires either a higher
   floor (which worsens OOS) or an entirely different gold signal (e.g. momentum,
   central-bank flow).

3. DO NOT RAISE THE FLOOR ABOVE 15%
   IS/OOS tension is monotonic: every additional floor percentage point above 15%
   costs more OOS Sharpe than it gains IS Sharpe. The 15% floor with compress
   is the efficient frontier within this parameter space.

4. KEEP THE 10-DAY WINDOW, NOT 20 OR 30
   10d gives the best combined IS+OOS score (3.000) and the best IS Sharpe
   (1.993). The 20d window gives marginally better OOS (1.010 vs 1.007) but
   worse IS. 10d is the better production choice.

5. SILVER SIGNAL UNCHANGED
   The GSR thresholds (T1: 83.36, T2: 86.45) and the 60-day peak/5% fall filter
   are not touched. Silver was OFF throughout the entire OOS period — no data to
   re-optimise against. The IS signal adds Sharpe; leave it as-is.

6. MONITOR THE COMPRESS SIGNAL IN LIVE TRADING
   The 10-day compress fires whenever DFII10 today < DFII10 10 days ago. This
   will trigger on short-term yield fluctuations as well as genuine trends. In
   production, check both conditions daily at close:
     CvsTC:     DFII10[today] < mean(DFII10[last 90 days])
     Compress:  DFII10[today] < DFII10[10 trading days ago]
   If either is true, allocate 25% gold. Otherwise hold 15%.
   Signal is lagged 1 business day before execution.
