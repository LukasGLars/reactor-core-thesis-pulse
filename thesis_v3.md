# Project Reactor Core v3 — Investment Thesis
**Framework: Short Abundance, Long Scarcity**
*Written: 2026-04-02 | Review: quarterly or after major macro event*

---

## Core Belief

The next decade rewards owners of things that cannot be easily replicated, extracted, or manufactured at scale. It punishes owners of things that are cheap to produce, commoditized, or dependent on frictionless globalization.

This portfolio is a bet that:
1. Monetary debasement continues — real assets outperform financial ones
2. Physical supply constraints matter more than financial engineering
3. Energy transition, AI infrastructure, and deglobalization create durable scarcity in specific nodes
4. Abundance businesses (cheap goods, high turnover, thin margin) face structural headwinds

"Short Abundance" does not mean short-selling. It means not owning businesses whose value proposition is cheap and plentiful. Costco was removed on this basis — a great business, but a beneficiary of abundance, not scarcity.

---

## Macro Backdrop

- **Monetary debasement**: G10 central banks expanded balance sheets 5x since 2008. Structural deficit spending continues. Gold and silver are the oldest hedge against this.
- **Deglobalization**: Supply chains are shortening and duplicating. This is inflationary for physical goods and favorable for domestic pricing power.
- **Energy transition**: Nuclear, grid infrastructure, and power delivery are the bottlenecks of the AI/EV buildout. These cannot be solved with software.
- **AI infrastructure**: Compute and power are the scarce inputs. Custom silicon and data center power infrastructure are supply-constrained.
- **Commodity cycle**: Decade of underinvestment in mining (2013–2022) has created structural deficits in uranium and silver. New mines take 10+ years. Price response is slow on the supply side.

## Position Theses

### Gold — 25% | Hedge
**Bull case**: Monetary debasement hedge. Central banks globally are net buyers. Gold has no counterparty risk. In a world of fiscal dominance, gold reprices upward regardless of nominal rates if real rates stay suppressed or negative.

**What has to be true**: Structural fiscal deficits continue. Central banks do not sustainably reverse gold accumulation. Real rates do not stabilize above 3% for multi-year periods.

**Role in portfolio**: Anchor. Non-correlated to equities in stress. Provides ballast when Vertiv, Broadcom, Lilly sell off together.

---

### Silver — 10% | Hedge
**Bull case**: Structural supply deficit driven by industrial demand (solar panels, EVs, electronics) consuming above-ground stocks faster than mining can replace them. Silver Institute data shows persistent deficits since 2021. Gold/silver ratio currently ~65 — historically mean-reverts to 40–55 in commodity bull cycles. If ratio compresses to 55, silver outperforms gold by 18% on ratio alone.

**What has to be true**: Industrial demand (solar, EV) does not collapse. Mining supply does not surge unexpectedly. Gold bull market continues (silver amplifies gold moves).

**Entry note**: Added at ratio ~65. Re-evaluate adding if ratio drops below 55 (thesis confirmation). Current position is a starter — not full thesis size.

**Role in portfolio**: Companion to gold. Adds asymmetric upside. Gold + Silver = 35% scarcity hedge. If commodity supercycle plays out, silver likely outperforms gold.

---

### Cameco — 10% | Cyclical Scarcity
**Bull case**: Uranium supply deficit is structural. Existing mines are depleting. New mines require 10–15 years from discovery to production. Nuclear renaissance is underway (France, Japan restarts, US small modular reactors, AI data centers seeking 24/7 baseload). Spot price must stay elevated to incentivize supply — Cameco is the largest publicly traded pure-play.

**What has to be true**: Nuclear policy does not reverse en masse. No sudden supply surge from new mines. Utilities continue long-term contracting.

**Role in portfolio**: Highest thesis purity of any position. If scarcity thesis is right, Cameco is the clearest expression of it.

---

### Vertiv — 10% | Convexity
**Bull case**: Data center power infrastructure is the binding constraint on AI buildout. Vertiv makes thermal management and power distribution equipment that cannot be substituted or produced at scale quickly. AI capex from hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta) is durable — these are multi-year infrastructure programs, not discretionary spend.

**What has to be true**: AI capex cycle does not collapse. Power infrastructure remains a bottleneck (no sudden commoditization). Vertiv retains pricing power.

**Role in portfolio**: Convexity to AI theme without owning AI itself. Benefits from scarcity of physical infrastructure, not software.

---

### Broadcom — 9% | Convexity
**Bull case**: Custom silicon (ASICs) for hyperscalers is a winner-take-most market. Broadcom designs chips that cannot be replicated by merchant silicon (NVIDIA) at the same efficiency for specific workloads. VMware acquisition creates durable software revenue. Networking is embedded infrastructure.

**What has to be true**: Hyperscalers continue custom ASIC programs. VMware integration succeeds. Broadcom retains design advantage over internal chip teams at Google/Meta.

**Role in portfolio**: Highest single-stock conviction. Benefits from scarcity of custom silicon design capability.

---

### Eli Lilly — 15% | Carry
**Bull case**: GLP-1 (Mounjaro/Zepbound) is the largest pharmaceutical market in history. Patent protection runs to 2033+. Manufacturing capacity is the binding constraint — Lilly is spending $23bn on new facilities, creating a multi-year moat even against patent expiry. Obesity + diabetes market is 1bn+ people globally.

**What has to be true**: GLP-1 patents hold. Manufacturing ramp succeeds. No competitor achieves superior efficacy at lower cost before 2030.

**Role in portfolio**: Largest carry position. Scarcity angle: manufacturing capacity, not just patents. Reduced from 19.7% to 15% to fund scarcity rebalance — still second-largest position.

---

### Walmart — 15% | Carry
**Bull case**: Pricing power moat in consumer staples. Largest retailer globally. Grocery dominance insulates from e-commerce disruption. Advertising business (Walmart Connect) growing 30%+ YoY — higher-margin revenue stream. Positive in 5/6 regimes including COVID crash.

**What has to be true**: Consumer staples pricing power holds in deflationary scenario. Amazon/Costco do not take structurally meaningful share.

**Role in portfolio**: Stability and carry. Reduced from 22.7% to 15% to fund scarcity rebalance. Provides ballast against commodity volatility.

---

### J&J — 6% | Carry Defensive
**Bull case**: Diversified healthcare. MedTech + Pharma. Near-zero drawdown in most regimes. Dividend aristocrat. Talc litigation mostly resolved post-spin of Kenvue. Defensive anchor.

**What has to be true**: Litigation does not re-escalate materially. Dividend maintained.

**Role in portfolio**: Minimum position for diversification and stability. Not a high-conviction bet — a portfolio stabilizer.

---

### DCA Reserve | External
**Purpose**: Held outside the portfolio in an interest account. Funds monthly DCA (~25k/month) and opportunistic deployment on meaningful drawdowns (-20%+ on core positions, thesis intact). Minimum 75-100k to cover both functions. Silver entry (~90k) funded from reserve when ratio <55. No structural cash inside the portfolio.

---

## What This Portfolio Is Not

- It is not a momentum portfolio. Past returns justify position construction but do not drive it.
- It is not a macro timing portfolio. No HY/IG spread triggers, no regime-switching. Simple DCA.
- It is not a diversification-maximizing portfolio. Correlation is accepted where the thesis is shared (gold + silver, Vertiv + Broadcom).
- It is not optimized for minimum variance. Volatility is accepted in exchange for thesis exposure.
